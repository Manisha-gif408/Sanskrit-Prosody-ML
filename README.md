🕉️ NaadBrahma AI: The Human-Vedic Synthesizer
NaadBrahma AI is a state-of-the-art generative engine designed to recreate the sacred resonance of Vedic chanting. Unlike standard TTS, which is linear and robotic, NaadBrahma uses Syllabic Matra Logic and Vocal Physics to achieve a "Human-Priest" quality that can bypass traditional AI detection.

🚀 Key Features1.

Syllabic Matra-Stretching (Prosody Engine)
Sanskrit is a musical language based on Matras (time units). 
Our engine performs a real-time linguistic scan to identify
:Laghu (ᑌ): Short syllables (1 Matra).
Guru (—): Long syllables (2 Matras).The AI dynamically reshapes the audio waveform to ensure Guru syllables are sustained with a $1:2$ ratio, mimicking the breath control of a trained Pandit.

 Bio-Acoustic Physics (Jitter & Shimmer)
   
 To achieve "Human" status, we injected two critical biological imperfections
:Vocal Jitter: Tiny frequency fluctuations ($5\text{Hz}$) that simulate the natural instability of human vocal folds.
 Vocal Shimmer: Micro-variations in amplitude (volume) caused by irregular lung pressure.

Svara-Mandala Analysis

The dashboard provides a deep-dive into the sound's "DNA"
:Temporal Waveform: Visualizes the rhythmic pulse of the chant.Power Spectral Density 
(PSD): Displays the "Chest Resonance" (Fundamental frequency around $120\text{Hz}$–$150\text{Hz}$) and its harmonic overtones ($f_1, f_2, f_3$).

Pronunciation Validator
 
A closed-loop feedback system.
Users record their own recitation;
the AI converts the audio (handling .webm/.ogg to .wav translation), recognizes the phonemes using a Hindi-Indian acoustic model, and compares the user's rhythm against the ideal Vedic Chanda.

🛠️ Technical Stack

Language: Python 3.10+
UI: Streamlit
DSP: SciPy (FFT), NumPy, Pydub (FFmpeg)
Linguistics: Indic-Transliteration (ISO to Devanagari mapping)
Generative Core: gTTS with Custom Resampling & Formant Shifting
